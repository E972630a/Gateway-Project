Activities To Do in Finland

Touring Finland and in need of some activites or events to do, don't worry for Finland has several activities.


Hunting for Aurora Borealis

Aurora Borealis is what is often called northern lights is a unique natural phenomenon. This spectacle occurs on a clear nights between March and September. The cost to see the natural phenomenon is free, but there are tours that will help you get a better vision.

Location(s): Lapland, Finland

Age Group:Any/Family Activity

Cost:Free


The National Museum of Finland

The National Museum of Finland gives visitors a story of Finlands independent years through its exhibits. It gives the visitors a prehistory of Finland and how it was settled 10,000 years ago. How the first settlers lived and more.

Location: Helsinki, Finland

Age Group: Any

Cost:$15.50 Adults
$11 Reduced*
Entry is free for those under 18

*Reduced entry fee applies students, pensioners, senior citizens, and persons over 65 years, unemployed, conscripts, persons undergoing non-military service and groups more than 10 persons.


Linnanmaki

Linnanmaki has over 40 rides, many different games, as well as restaurants and cafes. The entrance to the park is free of charge. You get to enjoy the atmosphere of the park, events and nine free rides at no cost! Even have a children's playground.

Location: Helsinki, Finland

Age Group: Any

Cost: The entrance to the park is free and 9 free rides to no cost. Any ride after that ticket prices range from $10 per ride to $46 for a wristband that allows you to enjoy all rides.


Ranua Zoo

Ranua Zoo is the northernmost zoo in Finland and the second in the world. The zoo's animals consist of approximately 50 wild animal species and 200 individuals, including top predators such as lynx, brown bears and wolves but also European moose and deers. The zoo has Finland's only polar bears in artificial snow.

Location: Lapland, Finland

Age Group: Any

Cost: Depending on the month of visit.
Adults:$16.50-$20.40
Children(4-14):$14.88-$17.60; under 4 free


All the prices were changed from euros to dollars.
